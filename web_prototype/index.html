Understanding the Body

The Adrenaline Spike

During panic, your brain releases adrenaline. It feels intense, but adrenaline is naturally reabsorbed by your body within minutes. This wave will break and dissolve naturally.

Hyperventilation

Feeling short of breath is often caused by taking in too much oxygen, not too little. Slowing down your exhale and prolonging it signals your nervous system that it is safe to relax.

The 'False Alarm'

A panic attack is like a smoke detector going off because of toast—the alarm is extremely loud and scary, but there is no actual fire in the house. Your body is reacting to a false alarm.
